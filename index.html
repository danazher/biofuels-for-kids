What Are Biofuels?

Biofuels are like super‑juice made from plants, seeds, or even tiny algae! When cars use biofuels, they puff out much less “yucky smoke” that makes Earth too warm.

Greenhouse gases Gases that trap heat around Earth, making it too warm. go way down, and our air stays cleaner!

🧪 Three Kinds of Biofuels

Ethanol

From corn and sugarcane.

Biodiesel

From used cooking oil and veggie fats.

Algal Fuel

From tiny water plants called algae.

Real DIY Experiment

(Only with adult help and safety gear!)

Step 1: Gather Materials
  • Veggie oil (250 ml) – kitchen fuel
  • Rubbing alcohol (50 ml) – helps it react
  • Lye (1 g) – magic powder to speed up the mix
  • Jar with lid & funnel – for safe pouring
  • Goggles, gloves & apron – keep you safe

Why? We need oil to turn into fuel, alcohol to help it, lye to start the reaction, and safety gear to protect us.

Step 2: Make Your Magic Mix

Adult only: Slowly add the lye into the alcohol (never pour water into lye!). Stir gently until clear. This makes “methoxide,” your fuel starter.

Step 3: Combine & Shake

Pour methoxide into the oil using the funnel. Close the jar and shake for 5 minutes. Then let it sit for 30 minutes to separate.

Step 4: See the Layers

You’ll see two layers: top = biofuel (orange), bottom = glycerin (yucky goo). Carefully pour off the top layer to see your fresh biofuel!

⚠️ For demo only: This biofuel is unwashed and unpurified—do not use in engines!

What Did You Learn?

  • Chemistry in action: mixing, shaking, separating.
  • Sustainability: turning waste into energy.
  • Safety first: why goggles and gloves matter.
